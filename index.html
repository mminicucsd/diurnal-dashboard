The April 6 Pollution Event
and the Tijuana River Corridor

A 13-sensor AirGradient low-cost network detected a significant PM2.5 and H₂S pollution event on April 6, 2026 in the South Bay / Chula Vista area. This dashboard presents the diurnal analysis, temporal rise and decay fitting, source back-trajectory estimation, and household survey context.

Key finding

The April 6 event shows two distinct phases: an overnight H₂S pulse (midnight–7am) originating from a known hotspot in the Tijuana River ~0.5 mi SSW of the trailer, followed by an afternoon PM2.5 event (11am–6pm) driven by WSW winds carrying emissions from the western estuary and river mouth segment directly over the sensor cluster.

Note on remote sensors

AG-5 (40.4 mi north, San Marcos) and AG-10 (30.5 mi north, Rancho Bernardo) recorded elevated PM2.5 on April 6 but are almost certainly reflecting a separate regional event unrelated to the Tijuana River source. All spatial analysis focuses on the local cluster (≤5 mi from trailer).

03 Temporal Rise & Decay Parameters Ciuzas et al. (2015) methodology

Each sensor's April 6 PM2.5 time series was fit with a sigmoidal rise function and double exponential decay (Ciuzas et al. 2015). Rise slope b quantifies onset speed; decay rates d1 and d2 describe fast and slow clearance phases.

05 Household Survey Context 9 of 13 sensors surveyed

Survey respondents reported building type, ventilation, stove type, window behavior, and occupancy. These characteristics help explain variation in PM2.5 readings across sensors on April 6 — particularly the contrast between outdoor infiltration and indoor source contributions.

06 Methods & References
Baseline period

April 2–13, 2026 excluding April 6. Hourly averages of 5-minute AirGradient readings grouped by hour of day per sensor.

Rise model — Ciuzas et al. (2015) Eq. 1

Ct = (Cmax−Cmin)/(1+exp(−(t−t₀)/b)) + Cmin
b = slope (h); smaller b = faster onset

Decay model — Ciuzas et al. (2015) Eq. 3

Ct = C1·exp(−d1·t) + C2·exp(−d2·t)
d1 = fast phase; d2 = slow residual (h⁻¹)

Source back-trajectory

Weighted centroid of 1-hour upwind projections from each local sensor, weighted by peak PM2.5 × (1/peak_hour). Wind from Quant AQ trailer anemometer (248°, 2.3 m/s).
